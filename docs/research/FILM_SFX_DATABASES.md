## Priority Downloads (Free, Massive, High Quality)

### 1. Sonniss GDC Game Audio Bundle
- **URL:** https://gdc.sonniss.com/
- **License:** Royalty-free, no attribution, commercial use allowed
- **Size:** 7.47 GB (2026 bundle: 347 WAV files); 200+ GB across all annual archives
- **Format:** WAV
- **Categories:** Cinematic impacts, foley, experimental textures, instruments
- **Why first:** Largest free WAV collection, explicitly cleared for sampling, annually updated. Download the full archive — it's the single highest-value free source.

### 2. 99Sounds Cinematic Collections
- **URL:** https://99sounds.org/cinematic-sounds/ + https://99sounds.org/cinematic-sound-effects/
- **License:** Royalty-free
- **Size:** 149+ curated sounds (Cinematic Sounds: 99 files + Cinematic SFX: 50 files)
- **Format:** 24-bit WAV
- **Categories:** Braams, booms, impacts, whooshes, tension builders, risers, atmospheres, loops
- **Why:** Production-ready 24-bit WAV, zero hassle, pre-edited for professional use. These are SP-404 pad-ready.

### 3. BBC Sound Effects Archive
- **URL:** https://sound-effects.bbcrewind.co.uk/
- **License:** Free for non-commercial use (commercial requires separate license)
- **Size:** 33,000+ sound files
- **Format:** WAV + MP3
- **Categories:** Foley (massive), atmosphere, mechanical, vintage Radiophonic Workshop recordings
- **Why:** Huge catalog, BBC Radiophonic Workshop sounds are perfect for sci-fi/retro aesthetic. The vintage recordings are unique.
- **Licensing caveat:** Non-commercial only unless you buy a license. Fine for personal SP-404 use, flag if Jambox ever becomes a product.

### 4. NASA Audio Archive
- **URL:** https://www.nasa.gov/artemisaudio/ + https://archive.org/details/nasaaudiocollection
- **License:** Public domain (copyright-free)
- **Size:** Hundreds of authentic space mission recordings
- **Format:** MP3 + WAV
- **Categories:** Spacecraft sounds, rocket launches, equipment hums, satellite chirps, radio emissions, Jupiter lightning, interstellar plasma
- **Why:** Perfect for sci-fi bank (Blade Runner/Alien aesthetic). Zero legal risk. Nothing else sounds like actual space.

### 5. Zapsplat
- **URL:** https://www.zapsplat.com/
- **License:** CC0 1.0 Universal (no attribution required)
- **Size:** 160,000+ sound effects + 650+ curated packs
- **Format:** MP3 + WAV (variable quality)
- **Categories:** Cinematic impacts, foley, sci-fi (spacecraft, robots, lasers), general FX
- **Why:** Massive, CC0 licensed, well-organized categories. Quality varies — cherry-pick the WAV packs.

## Secondary Sources (Check When You Have Time)

### Internet Archive Film Sound Collections
- **URL:** https://archive.org/details/various-sound-effects
- **License:** Variable (check each collection); many public domain
- **Notable collections:**
  - Universal Studios SFX Library (5 CD, 1,074 sounds from classic films)
  - Valentino Sound Effects Library (6 DVDs, professional studio quality, 1932-present)
  - William Dyer Foley Library
  - 1001 Sound Effects (comprehensive general library)
- **Format:** WAV
- **Why:** Historical film foley from the actual era of blaxploitation, kung fu, and classic sci-fi. The Valentino library spans 1932-present.

### Sonniss Anime Sci-Fi SFX Pack
- **URL:** https://sonniss.com/sound-effects/anime-sci-fi-sound-effects-pack/
- **License:** Royalty-free, commercial use
- **Size:** 499 sound effects
- **Format:** WAV
- **Categories:** Lasers, energy, ambience, spaceship, weapons, UI, anime-specific sounds
- **Why:** Direct match for Cowboy Bebop/FLCL aesthetic. Complements NASA archive for sci-fi bank.

### BOOM Library Free Cinematic Series
- **URL:** https://www.boomlibrary.com/sound-effects/free-sounds-cinematic-series/
- **License:** Royalty-free
- **Format:** 96 kHz/24-bit WAV (premium quality)
- **Categories:** Cinematic impacts, sci-fi elements
- **Why:** Ultra-high quality free samples. 96kHz is overkill for SP-404 (downsamples to 44.1kHz) but the source quality is excellent.

### Pixabay Sound Effects
- **URL:** https://pixabay.com/sound-effects/
- **License:** Royalty-free, no attribution
- **Size:** 120,000+ sound effects
- **Format:** MP3 only
- **Why:** Massive, zero-hassle licensing. MP3-only is the downside — acceptable for lo-fi/degraded aesthetic, not ideal for clean pads.

### Freesound.org (Already Integrated)
- **URL:** https://freesound.org/
- **License:** CC (varies by uploader)
- **Notes:** Worth doing targeted searches for: "film noir foley," "blaxploitation," "kung fu impact," "sci-fi ambient," "vinyl projector."

## Licensing Red Flags
- **BBC Archive:** Non-commercial only (unless licensed). Fine for personal SP-404 use.
- **Freesound:** Varies by uploader — always check CC license type before sampling.
- **Internet Archive:** Variable per collection — verify each before use.
- **Everything else listed:** Explicitly cleared for commercial music production.

---

## Audio Format Notes for SP-404
- Target: WAV sources first (Sonniss, 99Sounds, BOOM, BBC)
- MP3 sources (Pixabay, Zapsplat some): acceptable for lo-fi/degraded pads, not ideal for clean
- Everything gets converted to FLAC on ingest, then to 16-bit/44.1kHz/mono WAV on SD card export
- 96kHz/24-bit sources (BOOM) are overkill but downsample cleanly
